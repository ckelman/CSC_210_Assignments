Essay on the Dying Web:

The Web is not dying. While apps are certainly becoming the mainstream way to interact with the Internet, accessing the web through a browser is too valuable a resource to die out. Other revolutionary technologies, such as the telephone communication, have lasted many years in the face of other emergent technologies. Granted, hardwire telephones are being phased out, but only because they offer no real advantage over something like a cell phone. Whereas browser usage still has many advantages over apps.

The old web may very well dwindle to a more niche usage, but those who can use browsers to their full capabilities will continue to use it. Personally, I could not imagine using only apps in my day-to-day life. Apps are certainly convenient, but they simply dont come close to offering the functionality of a browser. The average person does not need all this functionality, however, which has led to the rise of apps that we have seen. The user-friendliness of apps, along with their convenience, could be likened to the explosion of popularity of Apple products as a whole. The OS X operating system is much more user friendly than Windows, which is exactly what an average consumer wants, even if that means paying more money. Still, there will always be those who stand by Windows, in spite of its shortcomings. This, in my opinion, will be the fate of the web browser as well.
